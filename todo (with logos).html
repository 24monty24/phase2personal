Culture

National Center for Civil and Human Rights

With Atlanta's history during the Civil Rights Movement it is only natural that the National Center for Civil and Human Rights would be located in downtown Atlanta. The Center is not just about history, but it acts as a bridge between the past and today's human rights issues.

High Museum of Art

Located in midtown Atlanta, the High Museum was founded in 1905 and has grown to become the premiere art museum in the U.S. southeast. Its more than 15,000 works of art include 19th and 20th century American and decorative art, African American art, modern and contemporary art, photography, folk and self taught art, and African art.

Sports

Georgia Tech Yellow Jackets

Atlanta is home to the Georgia Tech Yellow Jackets. Georgia Tech was founded in 1885 and has been playing intercollegiate sports since 1895. Depending on the time of your visit you should stop by and soak up the traditions of a football game at Bobby Dodd Stadium at Historic Grant Field, watch future MLB All-Stars at Russ Chandler Stadium or enjoy the ear-piercing excitement of a volleyball game at O'Keefe Gymnasium.

Professional Sports

Being the metropolitan city it is, Atlanta has a team in most professional leagues. There is the Atlanta Braves (MLB), United FC (MLS), Hawks (NBA), Falcons (NFL), Dream (WNBA), and the newest member of the professional ranks is the NLL Georgia Swarm.
